# 🎯 How LeadPilot Grades a Call
### in plain English · 2-minute read · no tech needed

</div>

---

## 💡 The big idea

LeadPilot is like a **coach watching game tape**. After every call, it quietly answers two simple questions:

```
   🧲  How good is this customer?     →  Is this lead worth chasing?
   🎙️  How well did the rep do?        →  What should we coach them on?
```

That's it. Everything below is just *how* it answers those two questions.

---

## 🏆 The one number everyone looks at: **Call Score (0–100)**

Think of it like a **final grade for the call** — a blend of three things:

```
   Call Score  =  🎙️ How the rep performed   (45%)   ← biggest slice: it's what you can coach
                +  🧲 How good the lead is      (30%)
                +  😊 The customer's mood        (25%)
```

> **Example:** rep did well (80), decent lead (70) → **Call Score ≈ 76**

---

## 🧲 The customer side — "Is this a real buyer?"

Four common-sense questions (salespeople call this **BANT**):

| | Question | In plain words |
|---|---|---|
| 💰 | **Budget** | Can they afford it? |
| 🪪 | **Authority** | Can *this person* actually say yes? |
| 🎯 | **Need** | Do they actually need it? |
| ⏰ | **Timeline** | Are they in a hurry, or "someday"? |

Each is graded, then added up. **More "yes" answers = hotter lead.**
The call also gets a simple label: **🔥 Hot · 🌤️ Warm · ❄️ Cold · 🗑️ Junk**

---

## 🎙️ The rep side — "Did they run a good call?"

A **report card with 5 skills** — the natural flow of any sales call:

```
   👋 Opening          did they build rapport, set the agenda?
   🔍 Discovery        did they ask good questions, listen?
   📣 Pitch            did they explain the product well?
   🛡️ Objections       did they handle pushback?
   🤝 Closing          did they lock in a next step?
```

Each skill gets a grade, added up to a score out of 100. **This is the coaching part** — you can see *exactly* which skill to work on.

---

## 😊 The mood meter

LeadPilot also tracks the **customer's mood across the call**, like an emoji strip:

```
   start  ▸  😐 neutral   😟 cautious   🙂 interested   😀 interested  ◂  end
                              "warmed up around 03:10"
```

So you can see *when* a call turned around — or went cold.

---

## 🧾 The best part: **every grade comes with proof**

LeadPilot never just says *"you scored 14/20."* It's like a teacher who **circles the exact sentence**:

> **Discovery: 14 / 20**
> *"Asked about budget, but never asked about timeline."*
> 🎧 *what they said at **02:35**: "What's your budget, sir?"*

Because every grade is tied to a **real quote + the exact second it happened**, anyone can play that moment and decide for themselves: *fair, or not?* You can **agree, coach, or argue with it** — with the recording right there.

---

## 🚦 "Still learning" labels (why you can trust it)

New grades wear a **"still being checked"** sticker until we compare them against real human experts on lots of calls. Until a grade proves it matches humans, it's shown **with a caution note and is never used to judge anyone's pay or job.**

```
   ✅ checked    →  shown normally
   🟡 learning   →  shown, but with a "still being verified" note   ← where we are today
   🙈 hidden     →  not shown at all
```

## 📋 A quick example

> **Call with "Rakesh" (in Telugu)**
>
> | | |
> |---|---|
> | 🏆 **Call Score** | **76 / 100** |
> | 🔥 Lead | **Warm** — interested, wants a site visit |
> | 💰 Budget | strong (₹80L confirmed) |
> | 🤝 Closing | weak — *"didn't confirm a date"* |
> | 😊 Mood | warmed up near the end |
> | 👉 Next step | **Send WhatsApp** with the brochure |

The rep sees, in 5 seconds: *good lead, your closing needs work, send the brochure.*
