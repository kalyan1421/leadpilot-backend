🏛️ LLM Council Verdict — LeadPilot AI Stack

Full Council (5 advisors · anonymized peer review · Devil's Advocate · chairman) · 2026-06-29 · Mode: Full
Council Confidence: MEDIUM–HIGH

Near-unanimous on the diagnosis (don't treat AI scores as ground truth; use 105B; trust Sarvam for Indic STT/language, not fine-grained reasoning) — but a strong Devil's Advocate legitimately reframed how much rigor is needed before shipping, which staged the recommendation by maturity.

The question: Is the LeadPilot stack — code, techniques, API strategy, product features, outputs — genuinely 2026 industry best-practice for production? Specifically: (a) sarvam-30b vs 105b for the analysis; (b) can the business trust Sarvam's AI analysis enough to show scores to telecallers/founders and decide on them? Stack: Python/FastAPI + Next.js + Postgres; Sarvam-only pipeline (Saaras v3 diarized STT → forced tool-calling structured analysis, decomposed + map-reduce → translation + memory; deterministic score aggregation).

✅ Where the Council Agrees (high signal)

  • The code & techniques are genuinely 2026 best-practice. Forced tool-calling (schema-locked JSON), task decomposition, map-reduce chunking, deterministic Python aggregation over LLM signals — all advisors treated this layer as sound. This is the strong part of the build.
  • Use sarvam-105B, not 30B — unconditionally. Cost delta is noise (~₹0.1–0.25/call vs ₹45/hr STT); 105B is materially better, especially on Indian languages. (Contrarian, Expansionist, Executor explicit; none dissented.)
  • Trust Sarvam for what it's SOTA at: Indic speech-to-text + diarization, language comprehension, extraction, BANT/entity tagging, code-mixed sentiment direction, and "what happened" summaries.
  • Do NOT present fine-grained numeric scores as truth. A 0–20 "Closing: 12" from a model that scores 18/100 on general reasoning is false precision. Attach the supporting quote to every score so it's auditable. (First Principles, Outsider, Contrarian converged independently.)

⚔️ Where the Council Clashes (real uncertainty)

  • How much validation/infra before shipping? The Contrarian/Executor camp says: build a human-labeled gold set + durable queue + paid tier before any paying customer. The Devil's Advocate says: that's Series-A infra on a pre-PMF pilot — ship directional scores with quotes now and build the gold set from production traffic.
  • Bands vs raw numbers. First Principles wants 0–20 collapsed into strong/ok/weak bands; the Devil's Advocate counters that banding may destroy the week-over-week signal telecallers and founders actually want to track.

🔎 Blind Spots the Council Caught (peer review — the most valuable layer)

  • Employment-law & DPDP consent exposure. Ranking/judging humans on AI output in India implicates consent for recording+analysis, the right to contest a decision, and labor-dispute risk. Flagged by 4 of 5 reviewers as the top universal miss. irreversible
  • Non-uniform WER → systematic bias. 19.3% WER isn't flat noise — ASR fails hardest on code-mixed, accented, rural-dialect, low-SNR calls, which correlate with specific reps/regions. So scores are biased against certain agents, not just noisy — and a uniformly-sampled gold set wouldn't even detect it.
  • STT→LLM error compounding. You can't score above transcript accuracy; nobody measured Sarvam's actual WER on your code-mixed telephony audio, and low-confidence transcripts should be routed away from scoring.
  • Goodhart gaming. Once the rubric is known, reps optimize to trigger keywords rather than sell better.
  • Drift + recurring eval cost. "Validated today" ≠ valid in 3 months as models/products change; eval is ongoing, not a one-time gate.
  • Rater reliability. A gold set with poor inter-rater agreement just launders human disagreement into a fake benchmark.

🥊 The Devil's Advocate Check

The challenge partially succeeded — and that's a feature. It correctly argued that the gold-set / durable-queue / de-precision bar is over-engineering for a pilot (BackgroundTasks survives dozens of calls/day; Gong/Chorus shipped directional scores with no public gold set; closed-domain QA ≠ open reasoning, so the 18/100 index is less damning than the Contrarian implied). But it conceded the load-bearing safety points: DPDP consent and a hard "never make a pay/firing decision on the score alone" guardrail are irreversible liability, not paralysis — and Goodhart gaming is real. Net effect: the recommendation is staged by maturity rather than "do everything first."

🧭 The Recommendation

Ship it — the engineering is 2026-standard — but never present AI scores as a verdict, and stage trust by maturity.

  • (a) 30b vs 105b → 105B. Settled. Use 30B only as a cheap map-reduce chunk-worker if throughput demands it.
  • (b) Can you trust Sarvam's analysis? Split it: Yes for STT/diarization/ language/extraction/summaries (Indic SOTA). Not as ground truth for fine-grained numeric scores — show them as evidence-backed, directional coaching signals (quote attached), framed as deterministic aggregation over schema-locked extractions, not "the AI's opinion of you."
  • Pilot (now, design partners): ship directional scores with the supporting quote; collect labels from production to build the gold set; keep BackgroundTasks. Velocity over perfection.
  • Before scale / paying volume: durable queue (replace BackgroundTasks), paid Sarvam tier (kill free-key rotation), contact_id DB index, per-dimension gold-set acceptance gate, drift monitoring, cap per-turn sentiment.
  • Non-negotiable from day one (irreversible): DPDP consent for recording+analysis; a hard guardrail that scores are a coaching aid, never the basis for a pay/firing decision (human-in-the-loop + appeal path); watch for rubric gaming.

🎯 The One Thing to Do First

Attach the supporting transcript quote (with timestamp) to every score dimension in the Score tab — this week. It's a small change that turns each number from "trust me" into auditable evidence; it's the single fix every advisor and the Devil's Advocate agreed on, and it neutralizes the false-precision and "rep can't argue with it" risks at once. Pair it with a one-line "coaching aid, not a performance verdict" + consent disclaimer.

🚨 What to Watch For (tripwires)

  • Design-partner reps dispute a score and are right >~20% of the time → that dimension isn't trustworthy; pull it to a band or hide it.
  • A score has no quote to back it → it's unfalsifiable; don't display it.
  • Anyone proposes using the score in a pay/promotion/firing decision → STOP. Human-in-the-loop + appeal required (labor-law tripwire).
  • Measured WER on your real code-mixed audio is materially worse for specific reps/regions → systematic bias; don't rank across reps until corrected.

Advisor full responses

The Contrarian: Sarvam's 18-on-the-index LLM is the fatal flaw, and forced tool-calling makes it more dangerous — schema-valid JSON is not correct JSON; a weak reasoner confidently emits perfectly-structured wrong scores. Scoring is pure reasoning (where Sarvam is weakest); Indic/STT dominance is irrelevant once the call is text. 105B over 30B unconditionally, but both are weak judges. Don't trust yet — you validated the pipeline runs, not that scores are right; one Telugu call proves nothing. One bad score kills trust forever. Before launch: human-labeled gold set (50–100 calls, 2 raters), measure correlation/MAE per dimension, ship only dimensions that clear a bar; mark others beta/hidden.

The First Principles Thinker: Solving the wrong problem — optimizing "a defensible 0–20 closing score" when the job is "help the team make one better decision per day." False precision on an 18-index model = the trust trap. Ask which outputs are load-bearing vs decoration. Trust transcription, sentiment direction, "what happened" summaries; not fine-grained numeric scores as truth. Collapse 0–20 into bands + supporting quote — the number can't be "wrong," only the auditable evidence. Then 30b-vs-105b stops mattering; spend the 105B/Indic edge on summaries and memory.

The Expansionist: The biggest upside isn't scoring — it's the keyword→ad loop becoming a proprietary, sovereign Indian-language intent graph nobody else can build. 105B everywhere. Trust Sarvam for extraction/classification/BANT/code-mixed sentiment; present the 5-dim score as deterministic aggregation over schema-locked extractions. Every 19.3% WER SOTA transcript is a vernacular dataset Google/Meta can't see; resell as QA/coaching, a telecaller index, or a fine-tuned vertical SLM; sovereignty is the BFSI/gov moat. You're building the intent layer for vernacular India.

The Outsider: The scores look precise but rest on a foundation openly admitted to be shaky, and nobody told the salesperson. One in five words may be wrong, then a "73/100" appears — why not 71? A rep with 12/20 Closing can't see what 11 or 13 looks like, so can't learn. The even 20×5 rubric smells arbitrary. Bosses will judge jobs on numbers built on misheard words + an admittedly weak reasoner. Build trust: show the quote behind each score, admit uncertainty, stop presenting fuzzy judgment as a hard number.

The Executor: Not production-ready. BackgroundTasks isn't a durable queue — a restart drops in-flight calls (data loss). 3 free starter keys / 4096 cap / unknown rate limits = billing+ToS landmine + 429s with no backpressure. Full-scan-then-filter DB dies at 50k calls. Batch STT + many per-turn LLM calls = unbounded latency/cost. Monday order: durable queue first; buy a paid Sarvam tier with backoff + semaphore; add a contact_id index + pagination; cap per-turn sentiment + set an SLA. Keep 105B. Don't ship until the queue and paid tier land.

Peer review highlights & tallies

Strongest (5/5 unanimous): the Contrarian — the only advisor who turned "trust" into something measurable (gold set + per-dimension MAE acceptance gate), separating "the pipeline runs" from "the scores are right."

Most-flagged blind spots: the Contrarian's own gap = production reliability (it never asks if the system stays up — the Executor's queue/DB/rate-limit risks). The Expansionist = monetizes/resells output it never validated.

Universal misses (what ALL advisors skipped): (1) employment-law + DPDP consent for ranking humans on AI; (2) non-uniform WER → systematic bias against specific reps/regions; (3) STT→LLM error compounding + nobody measured real WER on your audio; (4) Goodhart gaming of a known rubric; (5) post-launch drift + recurring eval cost; (6) rater reliability of the gold set itself.

Devil's Advocate challenge (full)

Point 1 (gold set): the hidden assumption is that measuring correlation requires labeled data BEFORE shipping — false. Gong/Chorus shipped directional scores with no public gold set; the quote makes the score auditable in-context. Build the gold set FROM production traffic. Point 2 (false precision): overblown — call QA is closed-domain extraction over a known rubric, not open reasoning; the 18/100 general score is less relevant, and banding may destroy the week-over-week signal users want. Point 4 (not production-ready): for a pilot with design partners, durable queue + paid tier + index is Series-A infra on a pre-PMF experiment; BackgroundTasks survives dozens of calls/day. BUT the consensus survives where it matters most: DPDP/labor-law consent and the "don't fire on the score" guardrail are irreversible liability, not paralysis — and Goodhart gaming is real. So: ship directional scores with quotes fast; skip the gold set for now; do NOT skip consent and the human-in-the-loop guardrail.
